--- SPECIALIZED PROGRAM (Month 18+) ---
Structure: 12-week block periodization cycle
Frequency: 6 sessions per week
Duration: 90-120 minutes per session

Phase 1 — Strength Block (Weeks 1-4):
- Heavy compound lifts, 3-5 reps, 4-5 sets
- Long rest periods (3-5 minutes)
- RPE 8-9 (1-2 reps in reserve)
- Minimal isolation work

Phase 2 — Hypertrophy Block (Weeks 5-10):
- Moderate weight, 8-12 reps, 4 sets
- 60-90 second rest periods
- Advanced techniques: Drop Sets, Rest-Pause, Myo-Reps, Supersets
- Train to failure on last sets

Phase 3 — Deload & Test (Weeks 11-12):
- Reduce volume by 50%
- Keep intensity at ~70%
- RPE 6 (4+ reps in reserve)
- Test new 1RM on main lifts
- Then restart the cycle

Advanced Techniques (for hypertrophy block only):
- Drop Sets (DS): Hit failure → reduce weight 25-30% → failure again
- Rest-Pause (RP): Hit failure → rack → 10-15s rest → max reps → repeat 1-2x
- Myo-Reps (MR): Activation set 12-20 reps → 5s rest → 3-5 mini-sets of 3-5 reps
- Supersets: Two exercises back-to-back, no rest (antagonist pairs)

   Includes: Coffee, tea, energy drinks, chocolate
   Source: pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24235903/

   Before (after 8:00 PM): Blue light can suppress melatonin by 30–60%

   Source: pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25535358/

   Claim: "Social jetlag" from irregular weekend sleep linked to obesity,
   depression, cardiovascular risk
   Source: pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28364486/

4. COMPLETE DARKNESS
   Claim: Even dim light during sleep reduces melatonin and disrupts deep sleep
   Source: pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35316830/

5. COOL ROOM: 18-20°C
   Claim: Core body temperature needs to drop 1-2°C to initiate deep sleep
   Optimal room temp: 18°C (65°F)
   Source: pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22738673/

6. NO HEAVY MEALS AFTER 7:30 PM
   Claim: Eating 2+ hours before bed allows digestion
   Large meals increase gastric acid, body temp, disrupt sleep cycles
   Source: pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26156950/
